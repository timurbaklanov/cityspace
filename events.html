Where to go in
Toronto

Van Gogh Exhibition

The Immersive van Gogh exhibition evokes the highly emotional and chaotic inner consciousness of one of the greatest artists of all time through an all-encompassing experience of large scale projections, art, light, sound, movement and imagination.

Book Release

Photo book documents Toronto artists entering the professional realm, struggling to survive while making an everlasting mark. Creatures Creating; a collective of artists and musicians celebrating its first event 10 years ago.

Egyptian Mummies

This exhibit organized by the British Museum is a rare and in-depth look at what life was like on the Nile 3,000 years ago. It features advanced CT scanning techniques, 3D images, over 200 extraordinary objects and six mummies. Closes Mar. 21, 2021.

Aga Khan Museum

Remastered opens new windows on the Museum’s world-class collection of Persian, Turkish, and Mughal Indian manuscript paintings. The Exhibitions team partnered with Ryerson University Library to create 11 interactive hubs.

Joan Latchford

Love isn't limited comprises select b&w photographs from Joan Latchford’s 60s & 70s era exploration of Toronto’s diversity and diaspora.

Christmas Trail

Canadian Tire hosts a magical, multi-sensory Christmas Trail designed to immerse guests in the sights and sounds of Christmas while staying in the comfort of their own vehicles.

S.T.A.T.I.O.N.

Guests at Avengers S.T.A.T.I.O.N. journey through an advanced, multi-room training facility, where they receive unprecedented access to the most sensitive intelligence and cutting-edge science behind the Avengers. Open during mall hours.

Arctic Lights

Janet Read's body of work presents “landscapes of consciousness” from a month’s immersion in high Arctic geography. Niagara Falls in Winter is an exhibition of acrylic paintings in which Gwen Tooth has experimented with multiple textures and glazes.
